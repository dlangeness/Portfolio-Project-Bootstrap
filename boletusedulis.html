Boletus Edulis

Common to the Northeast but has been found as far south as Florida. This mushroom forms mycorrhizal relationships with the roots of various trees, especially spruces. Cap is roughly 2-4 inches across, initially convex but becoming nearly flat in maturity. Stem is 2-4 inches in length, 1-1.5 inches thick, and is club shaped. Inner flesh is white and doesn't bruise. It's spore print is light brown to olive colored. Sometimes confused with the mildly poisonous Rubroboletus satanas which is also known as Satan's Bolete
